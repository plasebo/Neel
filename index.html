Our 125th... and Counting!

    The start and evolution of Ganapati Celebrations by the Bhiwajee Family in Mauritius

    Scroll to learn more

    Beewan Bhiwajee Rane was among the first generation of Indians born in Mauritius.

    His parents (Govind and Ganga) who hailed from Sawantwadi, Maharashtra came as indentured laborers and were working in the region of 7 Cascades/Magenta.

    Married to Cassee Gopal, the couple could not conceive a child in their initial marriage life.

    They thus laid their wish in the faith of Ganapati Bappa and made a promise that they would start celebrating Ganesh Chaturthi at their residence should their wish be fulfilled.

    On 15/04/1894, their first child, Govind Bhiwajee (commonly known as Jilloo) was born. From that year onwards the couple thus started Ganapati celebrations in their home at the foot of the 7 Cascades valley.

    Over the years, 2 new offsprings were added to the family namely, Baban and Nanoo and the family continued their faith in Bappa. Rituals like Bhoopali, Aarti, Lontangan and Visarjan were performed with the collaboration of family members, relatives and neighbours.

    During night time, to keep everyone entertained, they would sing and dance together around the famous jhakri in the praise of Bappa.

    Bhiwajee grew up as a strict and disciplined fellow and ensured that this vibrant family tradition is kept going.

    He would also encourage youngsters of the 7 Cascades Valley to perform Bhajans, Slokas, jhakri and lotangan during the festival.

    After the demise of Govind Bhiwajee, his son Isnoo Bhiwajee and his nephew Anunta Bhiwajee took the reigns of the family. They both mastered the art of singing and dancing jhakris not only within the family, but would go from village to village to perform along with their cousins and friends.

    Ganapati celebrations continued over the years at the residence of the family who had moved to the village of Henrietta by then (where now Mr. Neelchandra Bhiwajee resides).

    In the 80’s Guruji Sadanand Bhiwajee, one among the eldest nephew of the family, gave a new dimension of this on-going family tradition. He started practicing puja vidhis as per Maharashtrian tradition which later became a standard for Ganesh Chaturthi Celebration in Mauritius. And since then, there has been no looking back.

    The Bhiwajee family still ardently and piously welcome Bappa every year in their midst, come what may. For the past two and a half decades now, Ganesh Chathurthi is being celebrated at the residence of Mr. Rukmangat Bhiwajee (commonly known by everyone as Babet) at Bhagat Lane, Glen Park. During the festivals, precious moments are shared within the family who ensures that no stone is left unturned to welcome both Bappa and His devotees in great pomp.

    With the blessings of the elders of the family, on numerous occasions, over the years, the divine idol was kept and adored by the family for more than 1½ days.

    Moreso, the Bhiwajee family’s Ganapati can boast of its pure silver jewellery with which it is adored every year.

    And this year, we, the Bhiwajee family will proudly celebrate the 125th consecutive year of welcoming Bappa among us at home. For us, He is not just a Murti we bring home and offer our prayers but is considered as our very special guest visiting the family and fulﬁ lling all our wishes, as Sukh Kartaa and Doukh Haarta (spreading Happiness and eliminating sorrows).

    Ganapati Bappa Moreea, Podchya Varshi lawkar ya remains the ultimate motto of the Bhiwajee Family for Bappa to visit us each and every year.
